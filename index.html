CHARACTERS IN NOLI ME TANGERE
CRISOSTOMO IBARRA Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin is a Filipino-Spanish descendent of a wealthy Spaniard. He was born and raised in the Philippines. Ibarra spent seven years studying in Europe during his adolescence. He had no knowledge of the events happening in his country but learned so much in Europe. Upon returning to the Philippines, he found out of his father’s passing and wanted to know what happened. He saw that in the 7 years he was away, there was no progress in the country.
MARIA CLARA María Clara de Los Santos y Alba is the daughter of Kapitan Tiyago. She is known for her beauty and grace. Maria Clara is the object of Padre Silva’s affection and many men and women admire her. She is looked after by her aunt, Tiya Isabel.
KAPITAN TIYAGO Don Santiago de los Santos is the father of María Clara. He is kind to the poor and to the wealthy alike. He is known for hosting lavish parties that is open to many people. He is a wealthy businessman in Binondo. A close friend of Crisostomo Ibarra’s father and to Padre Damaso as well.
PADRE DAMASO Dámaso Verdolagas (also known as Padre Dámaso, Padre Damaso, or Father Damaso) was a Franciscan priest who served as the kura paroko of San Diego's parish church for twenty years before being succeeded by Padre Salvi. Padre Damaso was well-known among the Ibarras, being a good friend of Don Rafael, the father of Crisostomo. He is has a dominant personality and seeks importance from the people around him.
TIYA ISABEL The sister of Kapitan Tiyago. She is the one who looks after Maria Clara. When Kapitan Tiyago hosts parties in his house, it is Tiya Isabel who attends to the guests like welcoming them and also with the preparations and the food.
